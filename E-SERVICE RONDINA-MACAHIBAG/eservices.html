DSWD e-Services

The DSWD eServices is an online application systems that aims to provide direct public access to DSWD frontline services through the internet and reduce the processing and approval time of DSWD transaction with the public.

The DSWD e-Services covers the online applications, processing and approvals of the following frontline services:

TRAVEL CLEARANCE FOR MINORS TRAVELLING ABROAD

Please read all of the requirements prior to your application (Basahing mabuti bago mag fill-up ng application form).

  1. REGISTER for an account online.
  2. LOG IN and access the Minors Travelling Abroad Application Form.
  3. Review the required documents.
  4. Complete the MTA form and upload scanned copies of documents.
  5. Check your application and submit it.
  6. Wait for a notification regarding the initial assessment of your application.
  7. Receive an interview schedule via email.
  8. Attend the interview at DSWD FO with original documents.
  9. If approved, pay the application fee and present the official receipt.
  10. The MTA Regional User will finalize your application and issue the Certificate of Travel Clearance.
  1. Only Field Offices NCR, VII and XI accept online application for Travel Clearance during the pilot testing period. (Tanging ang Field Office NCR, VII at XI ang tatanggap ng online na aplikasyon para sa Travel Clearance habang nasa pilot testing period pa lamang ito.)
  2. Your application will only be processed during office hours, Monday to Friday, 8:00am – 5:00pm. (Ang inyong aplikasyon ay ipoproseso lamang mula Lunes hanggang Biyernes, 8:00 ng umaga – 5:00 ng hapon.)
  3. No online application will be processed and/or accepted if travel is within 24 hours. (Walang online na aplikasyon ang mapoproseso at / o tatanggapin kung ito ay kailangan nang gamitin sa loob ng 24 na oras.)

    The DSWD shall collect a processing fee for each travel clearance issued to minors travelling abroad under the following options: (Ang DSWD ay mangongolekta ng bayad sa pagpoproseso para sa bawat travel clearance na ibinigay sa mga menor de edad na maglalakbay sa ibang bansa sa ilalim ng mga sumusunod na pagpipilian:)

  1. The processing fee for securing a Travel Clearance is ₱300.00 for one year validity.
  2. Additional fees may apply for rush processing.

    General requirements:

  1. Completed Online Application Form
  2. Scanned Copy of Minor's Birth Certificate (from PSA)
  3. Notarized Affidavit or Consent from both parents/guardian permitting travel.
  4. Two Original Passport Size Photos of the minor
  5. Financial Support Affidavit and evidence of financial capability (e.g., employment certificate, bank statement).
  6. Original Copy of Previous Travel Clearance (if applicable).
  7. Copy of Traveling Companion’s Passport (if applicable).
  8. Unaccompanied Minor Certificate from the airline(if applicable).
  9. Waiver releasing DSWD from liability.


    Additional Requirements Based on Circumstances:

  1. For Minors Traveling Alone or with Non-Guardian:

    2. Affidavit of Support and financial documents.

  2. For Minors Under Guardian Care:

    3. Marriage certificate, court order for guardianship, or documentation for solo parents.

  3. For Seafarer Parents:

    4. Employment certification and copy of the Seaman’s Book.

  4. For Deceased Parents:

    5. Copy of the death certificate.

  5. For Missing Parents:

    6. Social case study report, blotter report, and registered mail return.

  6. For Foster Care Minors:

    7. Notarized affidavits, placement authority documentation, and certification from DSWD..

  7. For Medical Purposes:

    8. Medical abstract and recommendations from physicians.

  8. For Inter-Country Adoption:

    9. Placement authority and escort authority from ICAB.

    The application for travel clearance, together with the required supporting documents, shall be submitted by the parent/s or legal guardian or a duly authorized representative to the nearest DSWD regional office. (Ang aplikasyon para sa travel clearance, kasama ng kinakailangang supporting documents, ay isusumite ng magulang / o legal na tagapag-alaga o isang kinatawan, sa pinakamalapit na tanggapan ng rehiyon ng DSWD.)

DSWD REGULATORY e-SERVICES

REGISTRATION AND LICENSING OF SOCIAL WELFARE AND DEVELOPMENT AGENCIES (SWDAs) AND ACCREDITATION OF SOCIAL WELFARE AND DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMS AND SERVICES

Please read all of the requirements prior to your application

GUIDE IN USING DSWD REGULATORY ESERVICES
  1. Public User to register (Magrehistro ang Public User o aplikante).
  2. Public User to log in and go to Registration, Licensing and Accreditation Form (Mag-login ang Public User at tingnan ang form para sa Pagpaparehistro, Paglilisensya at Akreditasyon).
  3. Applicant to prepare scanned documents required before proceeding (Ihanda ng Aplikante ang mga scanned na dokumento bago magpatuloy sa kasunod na hakbang).
  4. Applicant to fill up all required fields (Sagutan ng aplikante ang mga blangkong items na nangangailangan ng tugon).
  5. Applicant to upload scanned copies of required documents (Mag-upload ang aplikante ng scanned copies ng mga kailangang dokumento).
  6. Applicant checks submitted application (Suriin muli ang kawastuhan ng aplikasyon at impormasyon bago isumite ng aplikante).
  7. Wait for a notification regarding the initial assessment of your application.
  8. Applicant receives notification of successful application through email (Makakatanggap ang aplikante ng abisong nagsasabi na matagumpay na naisumite ang application).
  9. DSWD Users to assess the submitted online application (Siguruhin ng DSWD User kung tuluyang naisumite na ang online application).
  10. Applicant receives notification through email on the status of the initial assessment of application: Approve, Reject, or Incomplete (Makakatanggap ang aplikante ng abiso tungkol sa katayuan ng initial assessment sa kanyang aplikasyon, kung ito ba ay Aprobado / Na-reject / Hindi kumpleto).
  • All applications received beyond 4:00 in the afternoon will be acted on the next working day (Lahat ng aplikasyon na natanggap makalipas ang alas-kwatro ng hapon ay mapoproseso sa susunod na araw ng pagtatrabaho).
  • Applicants may log in to their accounts to check the status of their application (Para makita ng aplikante ang katayuan ng kanyang aplikasyon, maglog-in lang sa kanilang accounts).
Processing fee for Registration, Licensing, and Accreditation
    The DSWD shall collect a processing fee for Registration, Licensing or Accreditation. For registration and licensing, the processing fee is One Thousand Pesos (1,000.00) each. (Mangongolekta and DSWD ng bayad sa pagpoproseso para sa Pagpaparehistro at Paglilisensya ng Isang Libo bawat proseseo. For Accreditation, an amount of One Thousand Pesos (1,000.00) per facility/program. (Para sa akredistasyon, mangongolekata ng Isang Libong Piso (Php 1,000.00) kada pasilidad o program).
Requirements of Application for Registration of Private Auxiliary Social Welfare and Development Agency at Social Work Agency (Ang mga kinakailangang dokumento sa pag-aaplay ng Registrasyon bilang Private Auxiliary Social Welfare and Development Agency at Social Work Agency)
  1. Duly Accomplished and Notarized Application Form
  2. Updated Copy of SEC Certificate of Registration and latest Articles of Incorporation & By-Laws
  3. Copy of any of the following (kopya ng isa sa mga sumusunod):
  • Handbook or Manual of Operations of its programs, policies and procedures;
  • Brochure;
  • Duly signed Work and Financial Plan (for two succeeding years) by the Head of Agency.
Requirements for Licensing of Private Auxiliary Social Welfare and Development Agency (SWDA)-Intending to Operate) (Ang mga kinakailangang dokumento sa pag-aaplay ng Lisensya bilang Private Auxiliary Social Welfare and Development Agency (SWDA)-Intending to Operate)
  1. A completed and notarized application form.
  2. One of the following: a manual of operations, a board of trustees profile, or a certified true copy of the General Information Sheet (GIS) from the SEC or a regulatory agency. Also needed is a notarized updated certification from the board of trustees and/or funding agency showing financial support for at least two years, plus a two-year work and financial plan. If operating in multiple regions, provide a list of all branch offices and proof of partnerships (MOA or similar). For each region of operation, provide a validation report from DSWD, ABSNET, or LGUs (unless the main office is in that region).
  3. If serving indigenous peoples (IPs), provide a photocopy of NGO accreditation from NCIP or a statement from the head of the agency confirming no IP beneficiaries. (Currently waived).
  4. If you have a past or current partnership with DSWD involving funds transfer, provide certification from DSWD and other relevant agencies confirming freedom from financial liabilities. If not applicable, provide a statement from the agency head confirming no funds/grants received.
Requirements for Licensing of Private Auxiliary Social Welfare and Development Agency (SWDA)-Already Implementing (Ang mga kinakailangang dokumento sa pag-aaplay ng Lisensya bilang Private Auxiliary Social Welfare and Development Agency (SWDA)-Already Implementing)
  1. A completed and notarized application form.
  2. One of the following: a manual of operations, a board of trustees profile, or a certified true copy of the General Information Sheet (GIS) from the SEC or a regulatory agency. Also needed is a notarized updated certification from the board of trustees and/or funding agency showing financial support for at least two years, plus a two-year work and financial plan. If operating in multiple regions, provide a list of all branch offices and proof of partnerships (MOA or similar). For each region of operation, provide a validation report from DSWD, ABSNET, or LGUs (unless the main office is in that region).
  3. If serving indigenous peoples (IPs), provide a photocopy of NGO accreditation from NCIP or a statement from the head of the agency confirming no IP beneficiaries. (Currently waived).
  4. If you have a past or current partnership with DSWD involving funds transfer, provide certification from DSWD and other relevant agencies confirming freedom from financial liabilities. If not applicable, provide a statement from the agency head confirming no funds/grants received.
Requirements for Licensing of Social Work Agency-Intending to Operate (Ang mga kinakailangang dokumento sa pag-aaplay ng Lisensya bilang Social Work Agency-Intending to Operate)
  1. Duly Accomplished and Notarized Application Form
  2. Pre-Accreditation assessment conducted by concerned FO (for new applicants only)
  3. Certificate of No Derogatory Record information issued by SEC (except those in operation for less than six months upon filing of the application) (Not applicable for public SWDAs)
  4. ABSNET Membership. Certification from the Regional ABSNET (RAB) President/Chairperson of the Cluster ABSNET (CAB) or the authorized ABSNET Officer attesting the active ABSNET membership of the applicant SWA .
  5. Notarized Updated Certification from the Board of Trustees and/or the funding agency to financially support the organization to operate for at least two years. (Not applicable for public SWDAs)
  6. Audited Financial Report of the previous year submitted to SEC and/or Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) shall be accepted; and/or Financial report based on the DSWD template shall also be submitted. For those SWDAs with a total revenue of less than Php500,000.00, an unaudited financial statement prepared by the Financial Officer and concurred by the Head of Agency, may suffice
  7. Profile of clients/community being served for the preceding and current year;
  8. Certification from the SWA’s Head of Agency on their observance and compliance to the provisions of Executive Order No.26 of 2017 (Providing for the establishment of Smoke-Free Environment in Public and Enclosed Places) and RA No.9211 (Tobacco Regulation Act of 2003);
  9. Manual of Operation
  10. Profile of Board of Trustees or its equivalent
  11. Profile of employees and Volunteers
  12. Certified True Copy of General Information Sheet (GIS) issued by SEC or any regulatory agencies;
  13. For applicant SWA implementing Child Placement Services.
    Certification from DSWD or photocopy of the certificate of training attended by the hired RSW related to child placement service
  14. For Center Based (Residential Based and Non-Residential Based) Copy of the valid safety certificates namely:
    -Occupancy permit (only for new buildings) or Annual Building Inspection/Structural Safety Certificate (for old buildings)
    -Fire Safety Inspection Certificate
  15. Water Potability Certificate or Sanitary PermitFor applicants serving within the Ancestral Domains of Indigenous people (IP) – Photocopy of NGO Accreditation from the NCIP.
  16. If none, certification from the head of the agency that has no IP beneficiaries.

    17. Temporarily waived until further notice.
  17. For applicant with past and current partnership with the DSWD that involved transfer of funds
    Certification from the DSWD Office and/or other concerned government agencies that the applicant is free from any financial liability /obligation.
    If none, certification from the head of agency that no funds/grants received from any of the government agencies.
  • NOTE:There is no need to get a validation report/certification of existence for the region where the main office of the applicant is located
